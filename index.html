My Balloon

I held a red balloon, its string in my hand,

It slipped from my fingers, like grains of sand.

It soared to the sky, so carefree, so light,

Dancing with the clouds, a beautiful sight.

It swayed with the clouds, a carefree delight,

Twisting and turning, out of sight.

Higher it climbed, so far and so high,

A speck in the distance, beneath the sky.

But soon, it was lost, so far from my sight,

A sudden pop, and it ended that flight.

What a waste of my money, I now realize,

Spent on a balloon, that met its demise.

If I’d bought some food, a meal to enjoy,

I’d have filled my stomach, instead of this toy.

Next time, I’ll choose what will truly last—

Something to nourish, not just pass.

This was inspired by a native folksong in the Philippines traditionally named "Ako Ay May Lobo" (I Have A Balloon) this is the translated version and extended of this famous folksong
